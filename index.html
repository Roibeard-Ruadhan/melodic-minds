What is music therapy?

Music therapy is a type of therapy that uses music to address physical, emotional, cognitive, and social needs of individuals of all ages. It involves creating, singing, moving to, and/or listening to music under the guidance of a qualified music therapist.

Who can benefit from music therapy?

Music therapy can benefit individuals of all ages and abilities, including those with developmental disabilities, mental health needs, neurological disorders, medical conditions, and individuals experiencing stress or life transitions.

What happens during a music therapy session?

During a music therapy session, the music therapist will assess the individual's needs and develop a treatment plan using music interventions such as playing instruments, singing, songwriting, movement to music, and improvisation. Sessions can be provided one-on-one or in group settings.
